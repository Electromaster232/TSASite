General Info

The Dictionary Definition of Cloud Computing is: The practice of using a network of remote servers hosted on the Internet to store, manage, and process data, rather than a local server or a personal computer. Cloud computing is often referred to as “the cloud.”

Cloud computing relies on shared computing resources rather than having local servers or personal devices to handle apps. In its easiest definition, cloud computing is taking services and moving them outside an organization's firewall. Apps, storage and other services are accessed via the Web. The services are delivered and used over the Internet and are paid for by the cloud consumer on an as-needed or pay-per-use business module.

Companies or individuals engaging in Cloud Computing do not own the physical infrastructure hosting the software platform in question. Instead, they avoid capital expenditure by renting usage from a third-party provider except for the case of 'Private Cloud' - see below. They consume resources as a service, paying instead for only the resources they use. Many Cloud Computing offerings have adopted the utility computing model, which is analogous to how traditional utilities like electricity are consumed, while others are billed on a subscription basis.

Fun Fact: The “cloud” in cloud computing comes from the practice of drawing a cloud to symbolize the internet in network diagrams.

Be Secure!

All cloud computing services (that are reputable) are entirely secure. Not even employees of the company can access your data. This is generally reflected in the company's Privacy Policy or Terms of Service.
